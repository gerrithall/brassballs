The setup

  1. When a second player accepts the challenge, a game starts
  2. One game is played between two players every day, and the result is emailed at midnight
  3. You can invite up to ten friends to play, which leads to a maximum of 10 games per day. There is a $1 buy-in for each game, so you can put up $10 in total

The game

  1. Players pool their money to create a pot. Each player then chooses one of two Brass Balls, one that means "Split", and one that means "Steal"

    • If both players choose a Split ball, the pot is split equally between them and they both get a bonus % (changing periodically) as a reward for being fair and splitting the pot

    • If one player chooses a Split ball and the other chooses a Steal ball, the stealer takes all the money minus a small fee and the splitter is left empty-handed

    • If both players choose Steal balls, they both lose their investment

  2. The table below shows how much of the pot goes to each player based on the different combinations of choices:

    3. Result P2 Split P2 Steal
    P1 Split 50%+bonus% 50%+bonus% 95% (5% fee) 0%
    P1 Steal 0% 95% (5% fee) 0% 0%

  3. The default setting is the “Split” ball, so players have to actively switch to “Steal” should they wish to do so
  4. The bonus % varies based on the number of people splitting and stealing, and changes periodically
  5. The players are encouraged to speak with each before making their choices
